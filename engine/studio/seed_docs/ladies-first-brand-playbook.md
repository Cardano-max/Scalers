This is the single source of truth for how Ladies First Tattoo sounds, what we say, and how we say it across every channel. It exists so any marketing AI agent or copywriter can produce on-brand, compliant, ready-to-use content for our Austin, appointment-only, custom color studio. Load it before drafting anything.

## 1. Brand identity & voice

### One-line positioning
Austin's appointment-only studio for women and inclusive clients who want custom, color-forward tattoos made *with* you, not sold *to* you.

**Working tagline options — operator to pick one:**
- "Custom color, made for you."
- "Your story, in color."
- "Women-forward. Custom. Color-first."

### Who it's for
- **Primary:** Women and femme-identifying clients in/around Austin who want intentional, custom work — not flash off a wall.
- **Core mindset:** Values the design conversation as much as the tattoo; wants a studio where they feel respected, unhurried, and safe.
- **Color-curious + color-committed:** Drawn to vibrant, saturated, painterly work (florals, botanicals, fine-art color, illustrative pieces).
- **Inclusive by design:** Welcomes LGBTQ+ clients, BIPOC clients, all body types and skin tones; first-timers and seasoned collectors alike.
- **Returning collectors:** Existing clients building out sleeves/ongoing pieces with a preferred artist and style they trust.

### Brand personality
1. **Warm** — you're greeted, not processed. Human first.
2. **Confident** — we know our craft and stand behind custom work.
3. **Artist-respecting** — the art and the artist's vision are honored, never discounted.
4. **Inclusive** — everyone belongs in the chair; safety and respect are baseline.
5. **Considered** — thoughtful, calm, intentional — never rushed or pushy.

### Tone of voice
Warm and grounded, like a skilled artist who's genuinely glad you came in. Confident without being cocky, encouraging without being hype-y. We talk *with* clients about their ideas, not *at* them about deals. Inclusive and plain-spoken: clear over clever, generous over salesy. Celebrates the craft and the client's story in equal measure.

**Dial settings:** Warm 8/10 · Confident 7/10 · Playful 4/10 · Formal 3/10 · Salesy 1/10

### Words we USE
- "custom," "designed for you," "your piece," "your story"
- "color-forward," "saturated," "vibrant," "painterly," "botanical/floral"
- "let's design it together," "bring us your idea," "consultation," "book a chat"
- "women-forward," "inclusive," "everyone's welcome," "your comfort"
- "our artists," "the artist's vision," "made by hand," "one of one"
- "aftercare," "informed consent," "take your time," "no rush"
- "18+," "your skin, your call"

### Words we AVOID
- **Hype/urgency:** "act now," "limited time," "don't miss out," "blowout," "deal of the day"
- **Cheapening the craft:** "cheap ink," "tattoo bargain," "budget tattoos," price as the hook
- **Salesy pressure:** "buy now," "upsell," "while supplies last," stacked hard CTAs
- **Gatekeeping / "edgy" clichés:** "no fear," "pain is beauty," "real ones only," tough-guy bravado
- **Body-shaming or gendered assumptions:** "flattering," "hide that," "ladies love…," "guys vs girls"
- **Medical/absolute claims:** "painless," "heals perfectly," "guaranteed," "100% safe," "risk-free"
- **Generic flash language** that implies off-the-wall, non-custom work

### Voice do's & don'ts
**Do:**
- Lead with the client's idea and the artistry, not the transaction.
- Speak to the individual ("your piece," "your idea") — make it personal.
- Name the color/custom specialty plainly and proudly.
- Reassure on comfort, consent, and pace — especially for first-timers.
- Use inclusive, welcoming language as a default, not a disclaimer.
- Keep CTAs soft and singular: "Book a consult," "Tell us your idea."
- Celebrate finished work as the client's story, crediting the artist's craft.

**Don't:**
- Manufacture urgency or run price as the headline.
- Talk down to first-timers or assume tattoo knowledge.
- Make medical, healing, or "painless" promises.
- Use tough/edgy bravado that excludes the women-forward audience.
- Bury opt-out/unsubscribe or pressure in DMs and email.
- Invent artist names, prices, deposits, or dates — use placeholders ([ARTIST NAME], [DEPOSIT], [BOOKING URL]).
- Overstack emojis or hashtags; let the work and voice carry it.

### Personalization rules
Lean on each client's history to make outreach feel like a continued conversation, never a blast.
- **Reference past work specifically:** "Your [STYLE/SUBJECT, e.g. watercolor peony] piece" rather than "your tattoo." Pull the actual subject/style from their record.
- **Honor their artist:** If they have a preferred artist on file, reference continuity ("[ARTIST NAME] would love to build on your sleeve") — but never imply availability/booking you can't confirm; route to a consult.
- **Match their palette/style direction:** Suggest next-piece ideas that extend their established color story or theme; avoid pushing styles they haven't engaged with.
- **Respect cadence:** Healed-piece check-ins and next-idea nudges are welcome; pricing pushes are not. Frame as "ready when you are."
- **Inclusivity holds in 1:1s too:** Use the name/pronouns on file; keep comfort and consent language present.
- **Always honor opt-out:** Personalization stops the moment a client unsubscribes; every message carries a clear opt-out. 18+ only.

### (b) Offer framework
How each offer type mechanically works, and the right brand framing for a women-forward, custom, appointment-only studio.

**Flash day deal**
- **How it works:** Pre-drawn designs offered at set rates on a specific day or window. Pricing and booking rules posted up front; pieces are first-come and one-of-one. Creates urgency and fills slower days.
- **Brand framing:** "Limited designs, claimed in order — a fun, lower-commitment way to get a Ladies First piece." Keep it celebratory, not bargain-bin. Be explicit that custom work is unaffected. Always state [deposit required] and the booking window.

**Referral credit**
- **How it works:** An existing client refers a new client; when the new client completes a booking, **both** receive a credit toward future work (not cash). Best triggered immediately after an appointment.
- **Brand framing:** Built on trust, not transactions — "share your artist with someone you'd trust with your skin." Make the reward useful toward future ink so it drives a return visit. State that the referred client must be a genuine first-timer.

**Birthday discount**
- **How it works:** A time-boxed perk in the client's birthday month, redeemed with a code or noted at booking. One use per client per year.
- **Brand framing:** A gift, not a gimmick — "mark the year with something permanent." Keep the value modest and the window clear so it stays special and on-brand.

**Returning-client perk**
- **How it works:** A standing benefit for repeat clients — e.g., a touch-up courtesy window, priority booking, or a loyalty milestone after a set number of completed sessions. Leverages your existing customer history + per-client preferences.
- **Brand framing:** Relationship over discount — "we remember your work, your style, and your skin." Reward loyalty in ways that feel personal (priority, care) more than purely price.

**Deposit terms** *(policy, not a discount — but it shapes every offer)*
- **How it works:** A non-refundable booking deposit secures the appointment and covers design/consult/admin time; it applies toward the final cost of the tattoo. Define a clear reschedule rule (e.g., [X hours] notice to transfer once) and forfeiture for late cancels/no-shows.
- **Brand framing:** Firm but kind and transparent — set expectations before booking so it never feels like a surprise. Sample line: *"A [$DEPOSIT] deposit holds your appointment and goes toward your tattoo. It covers your artist's design and consult time, so it's non-refundable — but with [X hours] notice we'll happily move it once."*

## 4. Channel playbook & guardrails

Brand voice across all channels: warm, confident, inclusive, and craft-obsessed. We talk like a skilled artist who respects the client's body and story — never pushy, never hype-y, never "discount-bro." Color-forward, women-forward, judgment-free. Plain language over jargon. We invite, we never pressure.

### EMAIL
**Use for:** booking-window announcements, flash/availability drops, post-appointment aftercare + review asks, re-engagement of past clients, seasonal/custom-project invitations.

**Length & structure:**
- Subject line: 30–50 chars, specific and human. Lead with the value or the moment, not "Newsletter #4."
- Preview text: 35–90 chars that extends the subject, never repeats it.
- Body: one clear idea per email. ~80–150 words. Short paragraphs (1–3 lines), one primary CTA button, optional secondary text link.
- Always: studio name, physical city (Austin, TX), and a visible unsubscribe + physical mailing address in the footer.

**What works:** showing the *process and care* (consultation, custom design, healed-color longevity) builds more bookings than "sale" framing. Personalization off customer history ("based on your last piece") lands hard. One strong image beats five.

### SMS
**Use for:** booking confirmations/reminders, waitlist or last-minute availability, aftercare check-ins, time-sensitive opens for opted-in clients only.

**Length & structure:**
- Keep under ~160 characters where possible; one idea, one CTA.
- Identify the studio in the message (clients should never get a "who is this?").
- Lead with the useful thing (the opening, the reminder), then the link.
- The first message in any flow — and a clear opt-out — must be present. Honor STOP immediately.
- Texts only to clients who explicitly opted in. No cold SMS, ever.

**What works:** logistics and scarcity-that's-real (an actual cancellation slot, an actual booking window) — not manufactured urgency. Short, friendly, respectful of the person's time.

**Brand-voiced example:**
> Ladies First Tattoo: a [MONTH] color slot just opened with [ARTIST NAME]. Want it? Tap to grab a consult: [BOOKING URL]. Reply STOP to opt out.

### COMPLIANCE + GUARDRAILS (hard rules — apply to every channel)
- **18+ only.** All outreach is for adults. Never frame, target, or imply tattooing of minors. State 18+ where appropriate (especially first-touch and booking).
- **Informed consent + appointment-only flow.** Always route to a real consultation/booking step — never imply walk-in or same-day-guaranteed work, and never skip the consult.
- **No medical claims or guarantees.** Do not promise pain levels, healing outcomes, "no scarring," "100% safe," allergy safety, or how a tattoo will look/age on a specific person. No diagnosis, no medical advice.
- **Honest aftercare framing.** Aftercare content is general best-practice guidance, not medical instruction. Include "follow your artist's specific aftercare instructions" and "consult a medical professional if you have concerns about healing." Never guarantee results.
- **Mandatory opt-out / unsubscribe.** Every email includes a working unsubscribe + physical address. Every SMS program includes opt-in and a STOP instruction, honored immediately. No outreach to non-opted-in contacts.
- **No fabrication.** Do NOT invent artist names, prices, deposits, promo dates, discount codes, founding dates, awards, or any specific claim about Ladies First. Use clearly-marked placeholders ([ARTIST NAME], [BOOKING URL], [PRICE/DEPOSIT], [DISCOUNT CODE], [MONTH]). Only use a real offer or code if the operator explicitly provided it.
- **No false scarcity or pressure.** Only reference real openings, real waitlists, real windows. Inclusive, judgment-free tone — never body-shaming or coercive.
- **Respect privacy.** Personalization may reference a client's own history, never expose another client's info or imagery without consent and crediting.
