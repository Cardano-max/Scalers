# Hook / CTA pattern library — tattoo (retargeted)

Content-repurposing + hook/CTA patterns, retargeted from B2B/SaaS sources to a
tattoo artist's organic social. Each pattern is a *shape*; the copywriter fills it
in the artist's voice (S2) and the result is gated by the AI-flagger (S3). Pick a
**different pattern per variant** — variety is the point.

> The human phrasing in the KB is the asset; the channels/ICP are not. Mold the
> pattern; never paste a template. (winning-strategies-kb.md, tattoo-applicability note)

## Hook patterns (the scroll-stopper)

| Pattern | Shape | On-voice example (mold, don't copy) |
|---|---|---|
| **Story-first** | Open on the client and the meaning, not the studio. | "She brought in her grandmother's handwriting from a recipe card." |
| **Reassure the beginner** | Name the fear, lower the stakes. | "First tattoo? Good. We'll go slow." |
| **Craft truth** | A concrete, honest line about the work. | "Fine line is unforgiving — one pass, no hiding a wobble." → (strip the em-dash) "Fine line is unforgiving. One pass, no hiding a wobble." |
| **Before/after-the-chair** | The healed result + the relationship after. | "Two weeks out and this one settled clean." |
| **Process peek** | One specific decision from the design process. | "We picked three plants that actually grew in her mom's garden." |
| **Question that qualifies** | A question only the right client answers yes to. | "Want something small that actually means something?" |

## CTA patterns (soft, on-voice next step)

| Pattern | Shape | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Invite the conversation** | Ask to start, not to buy. | "DM me to start your design." |
| **Free-consult nudge** | Lead with the no-pressure consult (approved claim). | "Consults are free — link in bio." |
| **Low-commitment ask** | Save/share/bring-the-idea. | "Bring the idea, even if you can't draw it." |

> CTA do-not: hard-sell, urgency/discount, "book now!!!", hype emoji. Keep it the
> way the artist actually talks.

## Repurposing chains (one angle → many on-brand pieces)

From one winning angle, the copywriter can produce a set without sounding
duplicated (tenegoacademy "omnichannel repurposing chains"; emilyxhug "content
repurposing"):

1. **The carousel/standalone split** — the same angle as (a) a story-first single
   post and (b) a process-peek post; different hooks, same pillar.
2. **Persona swap** — the same angle aimed at the first-tattoo planner vs the
   collector; the fear/desire changes, the claim set doesn't.
3. **Format swap** — caption-led vs question-led opener for the same idea.

Each output of a chain is still a distinct variant (distinct hook) and is gated
independently.

## Patterns to AVOID (they read as ads / AI)

- Rule-of-three slogans ("clean, bold, timeless") — flagged by S3.
- Contrast framing ("it's not just a tattoo, it's a statement") — flagged by S3.
- Generic openers ("In today's world…", "Let's face it…") — flagged by S3.
- Hype stacks (🔥💯✨) and 20-tag hashtag walls — off-voice for most artists.

People don't open the app looking for ads; the posts that win blend into the feed
and feel like something worth watching even without the product attached.
