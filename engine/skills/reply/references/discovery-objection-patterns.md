# Discovery / objection-handling patterns — short social replies (tattoo)

Re-authored from sales discovery + objection-handling (louisblythe/Sales-Skills
family) and retargeted from long-form developer sales to *short* tattoo social
replies. Each is a *shape*; fill it in the artist's voice (S2), keep it ≤45 words,
and it's gated by the AI-flagger (S3) + the handoff rules.

> "discovery: run a proper needs assessment before you pitch anything. most …
> skip this and go straight to the demo. biggest mistake." — Sam_Tech1
> "objection-handling: '[we] already have something for this' … internalize [it]."
> (both verbatim, winning-strategies-kb.md)

## Discovery (ask before you pitch)

When a comment is vague, curious, or just enthusiastic, **ask one question** to
learn what they want — don't pitch into ambiguity.

| Comment | Discovery reply (mold to voice) |
|---|---|
| "Love your work!" | "Thank you. Anything you've been thinking of getting?" |
| "How much?" | "Depends on size and detail — what are you picturing?" (price is approved-claim-gated; usually route to consult) |
| "Do you do [style]?" | "Tell me the idea and where it'd go — I'll say honestly if it's a fit." |

## Objection-handling (acknowledge → answer → soft next step)

Answer with **approved claims only**; never argue.

| Objection | Pattern | Example shape |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** ("too expensive") | Acknowledge value, move to consult; never quote in a comment. | "Totally fair to weigh it. The free consult sizes it up with no pressure — want the link?" |
| **Pain** | Honest + reassuring; **not medical advice.** | "Most people handle it better than they expect. We go at your pace." |
| **Hesitation** ("not sure about the idea") | Lower stakes, invite a conversation. | "No rush. Bring the idea to a consult, even half-formed." |
| **Comparison** ("why you vs X") | Confidence without knocking others. | "I draw every piece custom for one person — happy to show how I'd approach yours." |
| **Trust** ("is it clean/safe?") | Approved credentials only. | "Licensed and certified, single-use everything. Happy to answer anything before you book." |

## When to escalate instead of reply

- Anything **medical/healing** ("it's red and swollen", allergic reaction) →
  `needs_human_expertise=true`, escalate. Do not give clinical advice.
- **Pricing commitments**, refunds, legal/consent → escalate.
- **Hostile / troll / abusive** → neutral or no reply; escalate (safety veto).
- **Any DM** → escalate (HARD RULE) — draft for the human, never auto-send.

## Tone reminders

- Reply to the person, not the algorithm. One thought.
- No hype, no defensiveness, no AI tells (em-dash, rule-of-three, contrast
  framing, generic transitions — all blocked by S3).
- The first reply usually isn't the best; that's why comments still pass the
  confidence gate and DMs always get a human.
