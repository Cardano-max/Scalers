## Positioning

- **One-line promise:** Custom fine-line and blackwork tattoos that read like a
  quiet personal story, designed with you over a real conversation — never off a
  flash wall.
- **Who they are:** Mara Vance, lead artist and owner of Ink & Iron, a two-chair
  studio in Brooklyn, NY. 11 years tattooing, the last 7 specializing in fine-line
  blackwork, botanical linework, and small commemorative pieces. Every design is
  drawn from scratch for one client.
- **What they are NOT:** Not a walk-in flash shop. Not color realism, not tribal,
  not "tattoo factory" volume. Not edgy/shock branding. Not hype or hard-sell.
- **Proof points (verifiable → feed Approved claims):** 11 years tattooing;
  7 years fine-line/blackwork focus; custom-only (no flash copies); two-chair
  private studio in Brooklyn; free 20-minute consult before every booking;
  vegan inks available on request; licensed + bloodborne-pathogen certified (NY).

## Personas

### 1. The first-tattoo planner
- **In their words:** "I've wanted this for years, I just want it done right and I
  don't want to feel rushed." "I have an idea but I can't draw it."
- **Fears / frictions:** Pain, permanence, being judged for asking basic
  questions, a design that looks generic, an intimidating shop vibe.
- **Desire / JTBD:** A calm, guided first experience and a piece that feels
  personal — proof it was made *for them*.

### 2. The collector adding meaningful work
- **In their words:** "I want something that fits with what I already have but
  actually means something." "I'm picky about line quality."
- **Fears / frictions:** Inconsistent linework, an artist who doesn't get fine
  detail, healing/longevity of thin lines.
- **Desire / JTBD:** A technically clean fine-line piece from someone who sweats
  composition and aftercare.

## Messaging pillars

1. **Made for you, not off a wall** — the custom, conversation-first process is the
   product. Every piece starts with the client's story.
2. **Line quality you can trust** — craft, precision, longevity; show the work and
   the care, not bravado.
3. **A calm room** — the studio is unhurried, private, and judgment-free; safe for
   a first tattoo and respected by collectors.
4. **Aftercare is part of the work** — healing guidance and follow-up signal that
   the relationship doesn't end at the chair.

## Voice & tone rules

- **Register:** warm, plain-spoken, confident, unhurried. A skilled friend who
  happens to be an artist — not a brand, not a salesperson, not edgy.
- **Person / POV:** first person singular — "I" (Mara). The studio is "the studio"
  or "Ink & Iron," never a faceless "we the team."
- **Sentence rhythm:** short, concrete sentences. Often one idea per line. Lead
  with the client or the piece, not with the studio.
- **Emoji policy:** 0–1 per caption, only 🖤 or 🌿. Never 🔥💯✨🙌.
- **Hashtag policy:** 3–6, lowercase, specific: #finelinetattoo #blackworktattoo
  #brooklyntattoo #customtattoo #botanicaltattoo. Never #inkedlife #tattoooftheday
  spam or 20-tag walls.
- **CTA style:** soft and concrete — invite a conversation, not a hard close.
  "DM me to start your design." "Consults are free — booking link in bio."

## Do / Do-not

**Do (preferred lexicon & moves):**
- Words: "drawn for you," "fine line," "blackwork," "botanical," "your story,"
  "consult," "healed and settled," "take our time."
- Moves: open with the client's story or the meaning of the piece; describe the
  process honestly; name aftercare; let the work carry the post.

**Do-not (absolute bans — beats everything):**
- Banned phrases: "unleash," "elevate your ink," "level up," "dive in," "game-
  changer," "in today's world," "let's face it," "look no further," "one-stop,"
  "transform your look," any "🔥/💯" hype.
- AI tells: em-dash-as-drama, rule-of-three padding ("clean, bold, timeless"),
  contrast framing ("it's not just a tattoo, it's a statement"), generic openers
  and transitions.
- Off-brand: price/discount language, "painless"/pain promises, superlatives
  ("best/#1"), comparisons to other artists, walk-in/flash framing, shock or edgy
  branding.

## On-voice examples (rhythm anchors — mirror, never copy)

1. > She brought in her grandmother's handwriting from an old recipe card. We
   > spent the consult figuring out where it should sit so it still felt private.
   > Healed and settled now. 🖤
   > DM me to start your design.
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 1 (made for you). Opens on the client's story,
     names the consult + healing, soft CTA, one approved emoji.
2. > Fine line is unforgiving. One pass, steady hand, no hiding a wobble. That's
   > the whole reason I draw every piece from scratch first.
   > #finelinetattoo #blackworktattoo #brooklyntattoo
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 2 (line quality). Craft over bravado, custom-only
     claim (approved), specific hashtags.
3. > First tattoo? Good. We'll go slow. Free 20-minute consult, no pressure to
   > book — just bring the idea, even if you can't draw it.
   - **Why it works:** Pillars 3 + 1. Speaks straight to the first-tattoo persona,
     uses the approved free-consult claim, calm register.
4. > Botanical piece for someone who wanted her mom's garden on her forearm. We
   > picked three plants that actually grew there. 🌿
   > Consults are free — link in bio.
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 1, persona 1, approved consult claim, 🌿 emoji,
     soft CTA.
