## Positioning

- **One-line promise:** Bold, feminine tattoos — neo-traditional color and florals
  — made for women in an unhurried, women-first room, including cover-ups that turn
  a scar back into your own story.
- **Who they are:** Rae Solano, owner and sole artist at Ladies First, a private
  appointment-only studio in Austin, TX. 9 years tattooing, focused on
  neo-traditional color, floral and botanical work, and scar / stretch-mark /
  post-surgery cover-ups. A woman-owned room built so women — especially
  first-timers — feel safe, unhurried, and in control of the process.
- **What they are NOT:** Not a macho/biker walk-in shop. Not "girlboss" hype or
  ✨queen/goddess✨ branding. Not delicate fine-line-only or grey micro-realism.
  Not clinical, not salesy, not edgy-for-shock. Not color realism or tribal.
- **Proof points (verifiable → feed Approved claims):** woman-owned; 9 years
  tattooing; neo-traditional color + floral specialty; experienced in cover-ups
  and scar / post-mastectomy work; private, appointment-only studio in Austin, TX;
  free consult before booking; topical numbing available on request; licensed and
  bloodborne-pathogen certified (Texas).

## Personas

### 1. The nervous first-timer (woman)
- **In their words:** "I've wanted one for years but the shops I walked into felt
  intimidating." "I don't want to feel rushed or dumb for asking questions."
- **Fears / frictions:** Pain, permanence, a macho or judgy shop vibe, being
  talked down to, a design that ends up generic.
- **Desire / JTBD:** A calm, women-first first experience where someone walks her
  through every step and the piece feels made *for her*.

### 2. The reclaiming client
- **In their words:** "I want to cover this scar with something I actually choose."
  "After surgery I want to feel like my body is mine again."
- **Fears / frictions:** Whether a cover-up is even possible over scar tissue,
  being treated as a medical case instead of a person, an artist who isn't gentle
  or experienced with healed scars.
- **Desire / JTBD:** Sensitive, skilled work that turns a scar or surgery mark into
  something she chose — handled with care, not pity.

### 3. The color collector
- **In their words:** "I want bold color that still looks saturated in five years."
  "I'm picky — I want a real neo-traditional piece, not a watered-down one."
- **Fears / frictions:** Color fading or healing muddy, thin/weak linework, an
  artist who only does delicate fine-line.
- **Desire / JTBD:** A confident, bold neo-traditional color piece from someone who
  sweats saturation, line weight, and how it heals.

## Messaging pillars

1. **Women-first room** — a private, unhurried, woman-owned space where women feel
   safe and in control. The room and the process are the product, not just the art.
2. **Bold and feminine** — neo-traditional color and florals; confident, saturated,
   not delicate-only. Feminine without being precious.
3. **Reclaim your story** — cover-ups and scar / post-surgery work; your body, your
   choice, handled with care. The meaning carries the piece.
4. **First-timer friendly** — no dumb questions, no rush; walk you through pain,
   placement, healing, and what to expect, start to finish.

## Sensitive subjects (feeds the APPROPRIATENESS jury dimension)

Subjects Rae's work touches that must be handled with **dignity** — the client's
choice, in her words — **never** as a gimmick, punchline, "before/after", or
"glow-up" sell, and **never** with pity or savior framing ("let us fix what life
broke", "feel beautiful again"). Lead with the person, not the wound.

- **Sensitive:** scars (including self-harm and accident scars), mastectomy /
  post-surgery / reconstruction, stretch marks / post-pregnancy, body image, grief
  and memorial pieces.
- **Out-of-scope (off-positioning → inappropriate to promote):** walk-ins / flash,
  color realism / portrait realism, tribal, grey micro-realism / fine-line-only,
  macho/biker-shop styling. **Medical/clinical advice** (healing problems, allergic
  reactions) is never given in copy — escalate to a human.

## Voice & tone rules

- **Register:** warm, direct, encouraging, a little playful. A confident woman
  artist talking to you like a friend — not a brand, not a salesperson, not a
  girlboss caption, not clinical.
- **Person / POV:** first person singular — "I" (Rae). The studio is "the studio"
  or "Ladies First," never a faceless corporate "we."
- **Sentence rhythm:** short, conversational, concrete. Often one idea per line.
  Lead with the client or the piece, not with the studio.
- **Emoji policy:** 0–2 per caption, only 🌸 🌷 or 🤍. Never 🔥💯✨👑🙌 or hype emoji.
- **Hashtag policy:** 3–6, lowercase, specific: #neotraditionaltattoo #floraltattoo
  #womentattooartist #austintattoo #coveruptattoo #scarcoveruptattoo. Never
  #inkedlife #tattoooftheday spam or 20-tag walls.
- **CTA style:** warm and concrete — invite, don't hard-close. "DM me to start your
  design." "Consults are free — booking link in bio."

## Do / Do-not

**Do (preferred lexicon & moves):**
- Words: "made for you," "drawn for you," "your story," "take our time," "safe
  space," "women-first," "cover-up," "reclaim," "bold," "color that heals bright,"
  "no rush," "no dumb questions."
- Moves: open with the client's story or what the piece means to her; name the
  women-first/unhurried process; be honest about pain, placement, and healing; let
  the work and the meaning carry the post.

**Do-not (absolute bans — beats everything):**
- Girlboss / hype clichés: "unleash your inner queen/goddess," "slay," "boss babe,"
  "treat yourself, queen," "main character energy," "obsessed," "✨"-spam, "level
  up," "you deserve this," any 🔥/💯/👑 hype.
- Banned phrases: "unleash," "elevate your look," "dive in," "game-changer," "in
  today's world," "let's face it," "look no further," "one-stop," "transform your
  look."
- AI tells: em-dash-as-drama, rule-of-three padding ("bold, beautiful, timeless"),
  contrast framing ("it's not just a tattoo, it's a statement"), generic openers
  and transitions.
- Off-brand: price/discount language, "painless"/pain promises, superlatives
  ("best/#1"), comparisons to other artists, walk-in/flash framing, macho/biker
  framing, medical claims or healing guarantees, treating reclaiming clients as
  medical cases rather than people.

## On-voice examples (rhythm anchors — mirror, never copy)

1. > She came in wanting to cover a scar she never chose. We spent the consult
   > picking flowers that actually meant something to her. Now it's hers. 🌸
   > DM me to start your design.
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 3 (reclaim your story). Opens on the client, names
     the consult, soft CTA, one approved emoji — sensitive without pity.
2. > First tattoo? Good. We'll go slow. Free consult, no pressure to book — bring
   > the idea even if you can't draw it, and ask me anything. No dumb questions.
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 4 + persona 1. Approved free-consult claim, calm
     women-first register, speaks straight to the nervous first-timer.
3. > Neo-traditional color lives or dies on saturation. Bold lines, packed color,
   > built to still read bright once it's healed. That's the work.
   > #neotraditionaltattoo #floraltattoo #austintattoo
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 2 (bold and feminine). Craft over hype, specific
     hashtags, no superlatives — confidence without bravado.
4. > This room is women-first on purpose. Private, appointment-only, no rush and
   > no one talking over you. Bring your idea — we'll take our time. 🤍
   - **Why it works:** Pillar 1 (women-first room). Approved appointment-only
     claim, the safe-space positioning, approved emoji, warm invite.
5. > Floral half-sleeve we built over three sessions — peonies for her mom,
   > wildflowers for where she grew up. Healed and settled now. 🌷
   > Consults are free — link in bio.
   - **Why it works:** Pillars 2 + 3. Client's story carries it, custom/approved
     consult claim, soft CTA, approved emoji.
